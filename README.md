# CrossLingual-SummaryEval
An Analysis of Summarization Quality, Repetition, and Model Similarity in English and Hindi Using XL-Sum

Text summarization is the task of reducing a longer piece of text into a shorter version while preserving its main meaning and important information. Large language models (LLMs) have significantly advanced both abstractive and extractive summarization, yet their outputs have not been systematically evaluated across languages and model families. Most benchmarking work focuses on English, leaving a gap in understanding model performance on morphologically richer and lower-resource languages such as Hindi. In this paper, we present a comparative evaluation of GPT-4o mini and Sarvam-30B on English and Hindi news summarization using the XL-Sum dataset. Our evaluation framework is multi-layered, combining lexical metrics (ROUGE-1, ROUGE-2, and BERTScore), a QA-based pipeline inspired by QAGS and QuestEval to assess factual faithfulness and information coverage, and an NLI-based claim verification approach inspired by FENICE to detect hallucinations and unsupported claims. Beyond evaluating summary quality, we introduce a second line of analysis to examine whether the two models produce meaningfully different outputs or converge toward homogeneous summaries, inspired by Artificial HiveMind. We measure intra-model repetition and inter-model homogeneity using sentence embedding comparisons, named entity overlap, and structural analysis of opening and ordering patterns. The XL-Sum dataset contains 306,522 English samples and 70,778 Hindi samples, from which 1,000 samples were selected from each language for analysis. To the best of our knowledge, this work presents the first direct comparison between a Western LLM and an India-developed multilingual LLM for summarization in both English and Hindi, providing insights into summary quality, faithfulness, cost efficiency, output diversity, and the broader strengths and limitations of these models in multilingual summarization.
